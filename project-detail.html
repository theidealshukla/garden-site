Green Haven Villa

South Mumbai, India · 2024 · 8 weeks

Project Overview

Green Haven Villa is a flagship residential landscaping project nestled in the heart of South Mumbai. The client — a design-conscious family of four — approached us with a vision of transforming a bare concrete rooftop and two flanking side yards into a seamlessly connected botanical retreat.

We began by conducting a thorough structural assessment of the rooftop slab load capacity and soil drainage potential. Our landscape architects then developed a zoned master plan that divided the space into three distinct micro-environments: a productive kitchen garden to the east, a secluded lounge garden in the center, and a native wildflower meadow strip along the western boundary.

The Challenge

A concrete shell with unlimited potential but serious constraints.

  • → Structural weight limits on the existing rooftop slab restricted soil depth to a maximum of 250mm.
  • → High wind exposure at the 17th floor required wind-resistant plant selection and concealed support structures.
  • → Salt-laden coastal air threatened many conventional exotic plant species.
  • → The client wanted zero-maintenance irrigation while retaining an entirely natural, hand-tended aesthetic.
Our Solution

Engineering nature to work within precise parameters.

  • → Custom GRP raised planters with perlite-blend growing media reduced load by 40% while maintaining full root nutrition.
  • → Coastal-hardy species — Plumbago, Sea Holly, Agapanthus, and Wind-resistant ornamental grasses — were exclusively selected.
  • → Concealed drip-irrigation integrated into each planter with a smart timer, reducing water use by 60%.
  • → Natural-finish teak decking and limestone planters created an organic visual language that hid all technical infrastructure.
